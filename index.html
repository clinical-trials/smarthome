Why you can relax

The honest answers to “but what if…”

Letting someone change the wiring behind your wall is a real leap of trust. Here's exactly how we remove the risk — before, during, and after the hour.

“What if my system isn't compatible?”

We confirm compatibility from a few photos or a short video call before you pay anything. If your system can't take a smart thermostat, we tell you plainly and you owe nothing.

“What if something goes wrong?”

We're licensed and insured, and our workmanship guarantee means if the install isn't right, we come back and fix it. You're never left mid-project.

“Will you damage my wall or leave a mess?”

We remove the old unit cleanly, patch what we can, and leave the area as we found it. Tidy work is part of the job, not an extra.

“What if I still don't understand it after?”

You use it while we're still there, we leave a large-print guide for your exact setup, and a follow-up call checks that it's all still making sense.

“Will the price change at the end?”

No. You approve a flat number first. If a rare wiring surprise appears, we stop and show you the cost before doing anything extra.

“Can a family member be involved?”

Yes. A caregiver or adult child can join in person or by phone, gets the same printed guide, and is included in follow-up — handy for coordinating from another city.

A few sensible questions.

Is the thermostat really included? +

Yes. Both packages include one premium smart thermostat, such as a current Google Nest model selected for compatibility with your home.

When does the price get confirmed? +

You approve a flat price before we begin. We confirm compatibility from photos or a short video call first, so the number you see is the number you pay for a standard install. Rare wiring surprises are shown to you before any extra work.

How does picking a neighborhood day work? +

Enter your ZIP above to see the days your concierge is already routing past you. Hold the one that fits and send the request — we confirm by text within one business day. It's a request, not a charge; there's no payment until the work is done.

Does every visit take exactly one hour? +

About one hour is typical for a compatible single-zone install. Complex wiring, older controls, or HVAC repairs can take longer. We explain that before any added work begins.

Absolutely. A caregiver or family member can join the visit in person or by phone, receive the printed guide, and be included in follow-up support.
